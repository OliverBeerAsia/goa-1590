# Historical Reference Sources

This folder contains reference materials for the Goa 1590 game project. All sources are public domain (CC0) and should be used as the foundation for historically authentic art direction.

---

## Primary Visual Sources

### 1. Linschoten's Itinerario (1595-96)

Jan Huyghen van Linschoten lived in Goa from 1583-1588 as secretary to the Archbishop of Goa. His published work "Itinerario: Voyage ofte schipvaert van Jan Huyghen van Linschoten naer Oost ofte Portugaels Indien" contains 36 detailed engravings of Goan life, created by engraver Johannes van Doetecum.

**Key Images:**

| Image | Description | Resolution | Link |
|-------|-------------|------------|------|
| Rua Direita na Cidade de Goa | Main commercial street / Market of Goa (1596 colored edition) | 5011x1961px | [Wikimedia Commons](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Rua_Direita_na_Cidade_de_Goa.jpg) |
| Market of Goa (1605 edition) | Black and white version | 2720x1288px | [Wikimedia Commons](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Market_of_Goa,_Itinerario.png) |
| Island and City of Goa | Bird's eye city view | Various | [Library of Congress](https://www.loc.gov/item/2021668295/) |

**Historical Notes from Linschoten:**
- Market operated only 7-9 AM due to intense afternoon heat
- Parasol-bearers provided shade for merchants
- Social hierarchy visible in dress and positioning
- City "inhabited by people of various descent: Heathens, Moors, Jews, Arabs, Armenians, Persians, Indians from the surrounding areas, Banyans from Cambia and people from Gujarat and Deccan"

**License:** Public Domain (pre-1900 publication)

### 2. Códice Casanatense (~1540)

A 76-page watercolor album painted by an unnamed Indian artist for a Portuguese patron in Goa. Many scenes bear the Portuguese inscription "tirado polo natural" (taken from life), indicating eyewitness accounts. Now housed at the Biblioteca Casanatense in Rome.

**Collection Link:** [Wikimedia Commons Category](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:C%C3%B3dice_Casanatense) (73 digitized images)

**Key Images for Game Reference:**

| Water Sellers | NPC character design, daily life | Browse collection |
| Cattle Drivers | NPC character design, trade goods | Browse collection |
| Regional Costumes | Character sprite reference | Browse collection |
| Merchants | NPC character design | Browse collection |

**Coverage:**
- People from East Africa, Arabia, Persia, Afghanistan, India, Ceylon, Malaysia, China, Moluccas
- Fauna, flora, and Hindu traditions
- Period arms and garments (invaluable for character design)
- Daily activities: farming, dining, bathing, commerce

**License:** Public Domain (CC0 1.0 Universal)

## Audio/Music References

**Period-Appropriate Instruments:**

| Type | Instruments |
|------|-------------|
| Portuguese Introduced (from 1545) | Lute, organ, guitar, violin, mandolin, trumpet |
| Indigenous Goan | Ghumatt (earthen membranophone), dhol, mridanga, tabla, shehnai, surpavo/konpavo flutes |

**Historical Context:**
- Western music schools established in Goa from 1545
- Organ-accompanied chants and sung mass from 1551
- Goan music retained traces of early Italian Church music
- The **Ovi** (wedding songs) existed in this period (precursor to later Mando)

## Downloading Instructions

1. **Linschoten Engravings:**
   - Visit each Wikimedia Commons link
   - Click on the image, then "Download" at full resolution
   - Save to `linschoten/` folder

2. **Códice Casanatense:**
   - Visit the [category page](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:C%C3%B3dice_Casanatense)
   - Download relevant images for character/costume reference
   - Save to `codice/` folder

3. **Maps:**
   - Download from oldgoa.org
   - Save to `maps/` folder

4. **Palette:**
   - Use image editing software to sample colors from downloaded references
   - Create palette swatches in `palette/` folder
